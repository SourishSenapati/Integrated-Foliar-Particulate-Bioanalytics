## 2. Failure Modes & Risk Mitigation (Market Contingencies)

To ensure success, we must ruthlessly calculate how SylvaNode could fail and engineer preventative measures.

### Risk 1: The "Free Alternative" Threat

- **Failure State:** A government agency (e.g., CPCB) releases an open-source "recommended tree list," making our software seem unnecessary.
- **Mitigation (The AI Moat):** A static list cannot calculate aerodynamic wind tunneling (street canyons) or live PM10 saturation loads based on $m^2$ plotting. We market the 6-Sigma PINN (Physics-Informed Neural Network) aspect. We don't sell "lists of trees"; we sell **mathematically guaranteed PM-reduction tonnage**, which developers _need_ for ESG tax credits. A static list gets them zero tax credits.

### Risk 2: Biological Unpredictability (The "Dead Tree" Protocol)

- **Failure State:** A user buys the recommended _Azadirachta indica_ Bio-Shield, but it dies due to lack of water or poor soil, causing a loss of trust in the AI.
- **Mitigation:** The OmniPlant AI algorithm incorporates an **APTI Safety Buffer**. We only recommend species with an APTI of >18 for extreme zones. For the B2G/B2B market, we partner with IoT soil-sensor companies. Our API triggers an alert: _"Shield Sector 4 soil moisture low. Water immediately to prevent 12% drop in PM filtration efficiency."_

### Risk 3: Hardware Sensor Cost Wall

- **Failure State:** B2C users refuse to buy expensive PM sensors to feed our app data.
- **Mitigation:** **HazeVision 6-Sigma Model.** Users just point their phone camera at the horizon. Our trained CNN evaluates the visual haze pixel-degradation to instantly estimate PM2.5/PM10 load, requiring ZERO hardware. We also integrate free, open-source localized OpenAQ API data as a backup.
